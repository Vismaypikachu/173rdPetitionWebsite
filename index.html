In Northeast Bellevue, 173rd Avenue NE is a main artery of the Tam O Shanter West neighborhood with many young families and members of the community walking their dogs, traveling to the park, or enjoying a walk. Kids walking to school and various activities traverse through this major corridor. Yet much of the neighborhood lacks sidewalks leading to cars, buses, emergency vehicles, and pedestrians often sharing the road and leading to unsafe conditions.

The need for sidewalks in the area has been identified by the city of Bellevue's Neighborhood Sidewalk Program . Currently, 173rd Avenue Ne has been prioritized in the top 30 or so sidewalk projects. Only 1 or 2 sidewalk projects are selected each year, so unless this project is reprioritized, we are many years away from seeing functioning sidewalks in our neighborhood.

Specific project: Connect existing sidewalks along the east side of 173rd Avenue NE from Northup to 19th.

The goal of this website is to bring neighbors together to sign a petition, engage with city council members, determine funding options, etc. to have this sidewalk project reprioritized.

Steps in Progress

The Sidewalk Program Manager will:

1. Order another speed study and volume study
2. Run the sidewalk project through the priotizitaion framework again
3. Request a cost estimate for the project to determine funding options
